# Interview Questions & Answers: General & Behavioral

## 1. "You've worked in C++ for over 4 years, but also PHP and Python. Which do you prefer and why?"
**Answer Framework:**
*   "While I appreciate the rapid development speed of PHP and Python for quick scripts and web backends, my true passion and expertise lie in C++. I love having direct control over memory, multithreading, and systems-level operations. Building high-performance, low-latency middleware and drivers in C++ feels incredibly rewarding, which is why I've dedicated the last 4.5 years of my career exclusively to it."

## 2. "Tell me about a time you had to learn a new technology quickly."
**Answer Framework:**
*   **Situation:** When I joined Mphasis, I was suddenly tasked with migrating Azure DevOps CI/CD pipelines and working heavily with Windows capabilities (UAP/Win32), which was a shift from my pure backend TCP/IP work at Market Simplified.
*   **Task:** I needed to quickly understand Azure pipeline templates, YAML, and Windows-specific driver compilation.
*   **Action:** I relied on official Microsoft documentation and broke the pipeline migration down into small, isolated POCs (Proof of Concepts). I also took the initiative to complete CS50 courses to solidify my foundational knowledge during this transition.
*   **Result:** I successfully automated the CI/CD pipeline, synchronized the DLL versions, and got the test suites to a 100% pass rate.

## 3. "Where do you see yourself in the next 3 to 5 years?"
**Answer Framework:**
*   "I see myself transitioning into a robust Lead/Architect role. Over the last 5 years, I've mastered the implementation of C++ backend systems, multithreading, and CI/CD pipelines. In the coming years, I want to be the person designing the high-level architecture—deciding *which* design patterns to use, *how* the microservices will communicate, and mentoring junior developers to write clean, SOLID C++ code."

## 4. "What is your biggest weakness?"
**Answer Framework:**
*   *Note: Always choose a real, but fixable weakness, and show how you are fixing it.*
*   "Because I am so detail-oriented, especially when hunting down memory leaks in GDB or fixing strict compiler errors, I sometimes get tunnel vision and spend too much time trying to perfect a small piece of code. However, I've actively improved this by strictly adhering to Agile practices—timeboxing my debugging sessions and relying more heavily on peer code reviews and the automated test suites I built to catch edge cases, rather than trying to do it all manually."
